ONLINE STUDY HABITS

of Izekiel David

Make a studying routine...

You have to figure out what time of day and which days of the week are best for you and make it part of your routine. If you study at the same time, in the same place, week after week, studying will become habitual, and you’ll have a far easier time finding the motivation to do it. It’s like taking a workout class. The first few times it’s difficult to motivate yourself to go. But if you go to the same class week after week, pretty soon it becomes part of your routine and you look forward to your workout.

Treat studying as your job to do...

When you are enrolled in your online program, studying is part of your job. You need to commit to your studies as seriously as you commit to your career. Take your online courses seriously and you’ll find success.

Make a Study Plan for yourself...

Sit down at the beginning of every semester and make a study calendar for the next few months. Write down all your major assignments, deadlines, and exams, then create a realistic plan to help you work steadily towards these important dates. Use this study plan to make sure you don’t fall behind on your online classes.

Create a private study space for yourself...

Dedicate a specific quiet space for your studies. Set up a desk with no distractions and turn it into a space that has everything you need for successful study time: empty flashcards, notebooks, pens, pencils, highlighters—whatever you need. Use this space for studying and only studying. It can either be your own bedroom or just a corner inside your house that where you will not be disturbed.

Take a break one moment at a time and even treat yourself...

Breaks are an important part of the studying process as well. The human mind can only stay focused for so long before it starts to run out of energy. Take a five-minute break every hour. Stand up, walk around the room, stretch, maybe go get some coffee or treat yourself with a snack, and come back to your desk refreshed.

MY GOALS FOR THIS SCHOOL YEAR

Short-term Goals

1. Pass all the requirements on Time

Assignments when submitted on a timely basis have a great impact on the overall grades. Managing all your work is a practice that can become habitual and is beneficial especially for you.

2. Making time for myslelf

Yes, studying and passing all the requirements is needed but never forget to make time for yourself. Students tend to be stressed out when it comes to finising all the tasks due, making time for yourself brings a positive feedback to you mentally and physically.

3. Be present at all times

Being on the class all the time is the right way but being present is not only on the physical aspect, you must be present mentally in order to learn the lesson that your professor is teaching. Make every lesson worth your time.

Long Term Goals

1. Focus on self improvement

Growth in yourself eventually leads you to new opportunities, opportunities that don't come about until you grow into the person who is ready for them. From relationships to careers – knowing your strengths is important for every sphere of your life. It gives you a better understanding of what you are seeking and where you are likely to thrive and excel.

Being a college graduate is just a stepping stone for me. Career goals wre the hardest for me to come up with after college. Being unsure of what I wanted to do with my life. Career aideas are not measureable, ther are dependend on our circumstances.

Having a fun life is about more than doing fun things and having adventures. You need to cultivate a mindset that embraces play, pleasure, and laughter.
